====== Damania ======

**LATIN NAME: **Turnera diffusa aphrodisiaca 
**RANGE: **Southern N. America to Northern S. America.

**PFAF ENTRY (Insecure Connection): **http://www.pfaf.org/user/Plant.aspx?LatinName=Turnera+diffusa+aphrodisiaca

**PARTS USED: **

* The leaves of the damiana plant are used for medicinal purposes. They are usually dried but can be used fresh as well.

**ASPECTS:**

* Anti-aromatase in high concentrations (see below)
* Anti-depression
* Aphrodisiac
* Anti-anxiety
* Diuretic
* Antiseptic
* Anti-inflammatory


**NOTES:**

* Although it is still found in the wild in certain areas, most of the damiana used in herbal remedies today comes from Mexican and Latin American herb ranches. ([[https://www.herbal-supplement-resource.com/damiana-herb.html|Source]])

* Although the plant exhibits some estrogenic qualities, overall it may increase the presence of testosterone, due to its inhibition of the enzyme aromatase. ([[https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18948180/|Source]])
